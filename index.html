About the Program

Farm operating loans, backed by government

The Advance Payments Program is a federal loan guarantee program which provides agricultural producers with access to low-cost cash advances that can help cover operating expenses and provide for potentially favourable marketing opportunities.

The program is offered under the Agricultural Marketing Programs Act, managed by Agriculture & Agri-Food Canada and administered by PEIFA. Program costs and rates are favourable for participating producers, and support is provided throughout the process.

Advances and repayments are processed electronically through PEIFA, which works directly with AAFC on your behalf.

Apply today by speaking to a PEIFA APP Program Officer at 902-368-7289

Zero Interest Under $250,000
Your full advance is interest-free — you borrow and repay the exact same amount.
Interest-Advantaged Above $250,000
Larger advances are charged at Prime minus 0.75% — still well below standard commercial lending rates.
Secured Against Your Commodity
Advances are secured against your crop or livestock — not your land, buildings, or equipment.
Electronic Processing
Advances and repayments are handled electronically through PEIFA for fast, convenient access.
On-Farm Inspections
PEIFA conducts commodity inspections at your farm to support the application and verify inventory.

Interest Rates

Competitive rates for every operation

APP advances are structured in two tiers based on total advance amount.

Under $250,000
0%
Interest-Free
The federal government covers all interest on advances below $250,000. You receive your advance and repay exactly the same amount — no interest charges at all.
$250,000 and Over
Prime − 0.75%
Interest-Advantaged
Advances at or above $250,000 are charged at prime minus 0.75% — significantly below standard commercial financing and a major advantage for larger operations.
Note: Administrative and security document fees apply to all advances, ranging from $675 to $1,875 depending on the advance amount and complexity. Contact PEIFA for a full breakdown before applying.

Advance Calculations

How your advance amount is determined

Advance amounts are calculated using a standard formula based on your commodity type. Select a tab to see the details.

Acreage × Guaranteed Insured Yield × Advance Rate = $ Advance Amount
Barley example300 acres × 0.998 × $145.50/MT ≈ $43,563
Table Potatoes example100 acres × 270.0% × $3.88/CWT ≈ $104,760
—Audited inventory calculations required by January 31st each year
—Sales invoices required upon repayment after January 31st
—Crop insurance cheques are delivered to PEIFA up to the amount advanced
—A 1% penalty applies for repayments made without proof of sale (POS)
—Latest date to repay is September 30th
—Default after September 30th incurs a 2% penalty rate, retroactive to day one
Number of Head × $ Advance Rate = $ Advance Amount
Beef Cattle example50 head (1,250+ lbs) × $1,500/head ≈ $75,000
Lambs example300 head (110+ lbs) × $145/head ≈ $43,500
—Livestock inspections are completed at the time of signing the application
—Advance rates are based on the anticipated future sales weight of the animal
—Sales invoices are required upon every repayment
—Producer must have 100% ownership of all livestock in the advance
—Custom feeding for another producer is not permitted
—Hogs and lambs are continuous-flow advances only

Standard Flow

Up to 24 months
May 2026 advance → due March 31, 2028
May 2027 advance → due March 31, 2029
For cow/calf operations — repayments made on an ongoing basis as animals are sold

Continuous Flow

Up to 16 months
May 2026 advance → due September 30, 2027
For feeder cattle (heavies) — repayments made at end of the advance year
—Beef cattle advance rates vary by weight class: 400–700 lbs · 700–900 lbs · under 1,250 lbs · over 1,250 lbs
—PEIFA can work with FCC to arrange a first-priority split of cow and calf operations, allowing producers to benefit from both programs simultaneously

Program Prerequisites

What you'll need to apply

Most agricultural products are eligible for an APP advance. However, supply-managed livestock and breeding animals not intended for market during the production period are not eligible. For full details, visit Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada's website or speak to an APP Program Officer.

Financial Records

  • Two years of audited farm income and expenses
  • One year of sales and purchasing invoices
  • Declaration of registered ownership
  • Provincial business license

Credit & Banking

  • Equifax credit check
  • PPRS creditor check
  • Financial institution priority agreement
  • Bank, Credit Union, FCC, FPEI, or ACL

Insurance Coverage

  • Registered and maintained crop insurance
  • AgriStability coverage for livestock
  • Commodity storage insurance coverage

Inventory Documentation

  • Maintained herd inventory records
  • Audited on-site inventory
  • AIC — Audited Storage Inventory

Livestock-Specific

  • Livestock inventory inspection at application
  • 100% producer ownership of livestock
  • Relatedness review where applicable

Need Help Getting Started?

  • Call PEIFA — a program officer will guide you through everything
  • On-farm visits available for commodity inspections
  • We can help coordinate with your financial institution

How to Apply

Four steps to your advance

Straightforward process. Our program officers Paul and Andrew are available every step of the way.

01
Contact PEIFA
Reach out to Paul King or Andrew Wood. They'll confirm your eligibility, explain requirements, and arrange a commodity inspection if needed.
02
Gather Documents
Prepare your financial records, proof of ownership, insurance documents, and inventory records. Our team guides you through exactly what's needed.
03
PEIFA Qualifies & Submits
PEIFA reviews your application, completes required checks, and submits your qualified file to AAFC under the program partnership agreement.
04
Receive Funds & Repay
Funds are deposited electronically. Repay with sales invoices when you sell your product, within your applicable repayment deadline.

Key documents to prepare

  • 2 years audited farm income & expenses
  • 1 year of sales & purchasing invoices
  • Declaration of registered ownership
  • Provincial business license
  • Crop insurance or AgriStability documentation
  • Herd inventory records (livestock)
  • Financial institution priority agreement
